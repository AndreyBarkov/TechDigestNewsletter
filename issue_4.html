Podcasts

Curated by Abid Hasan

 

 

 Uber Mobile Engineering: Distributed Payment Systems with Gergely Orosz  (52:16)

 

Listen to some thoughts from an ex-Uber software engineer on achieving high availability, lower latency, and fault tolerance for payment processing systems at the Uber scale. Discusses the challenges surrounding something as critical as payments along with some strategies to overcome them at Uber.

 

 


 

 

 

 

Software and the Law with Mark Radcliffe  (50:52)

 

This one discusses legal matters around the software industry from a lawyer who has been a part of it for many decades. Mark is an eloquent speaker and highly knowledgeable. He discusses traditional software licensing models, changes introduced with cloud computing, cryptocurrency/blockchain, etc.

 

 


 

 

 

 

Twitter Search with Nico Tonozzi  (44:40)

 

Twitter software engineer discusses creating and optimizing a search index at the Twitter scale, including the challenges encountered in managing explosive growth, indexing in real-time, and attaining high service availability (TL;DR: they settled on Lucene as the search index).

 


 

 

 

 

 

Articles

 

 

 

A series of nonverbal algorithm assembly instructions

by Sandor P. Fekete and Sebastian Morr

 

 

 

 

Have you ever been in a situation that you need to delegate the implementation of a particular algorithm but don't have time to explain? Say no more. Use this fantastic website with Ikea-style assembly instructions for some commonly used algorithms to have peace of mind.

 

On a serious note, it is an excellent example of turning something that could be a whole bunch of code and text snippets into an entertaining piece of info. Maybe it will inspire you to be creative while writing your next internal wiki page.

 


 

 

 

 

 

Infographic: The Rise of No-Code Development Platforms

 

 

 

 

No-code tools like PowerApps have been around for some time, and now they are getting more and more attention. This great infographic sums app key points about using No-Code platforms and how it affects development cycle efficiency.

Videos

 

 

 

Spotify Engineering Culture (13:12)

by Henrik Kniberg

 

 

 

 

 

Nowadays, all development is striving to be Agile. Some various flavors and frameworks fall under the Agile umbrella. However, each development project is different, and there is no Agile methodology that can handle all real-life scenarios. Fortunately, the answer to this is kind of in the name. You can be agile in your development process, meaning pick all the best things that work for your team from every framework and get rid of everything that doesn't work.

 

Here's a video on how Spotify adapted Agile philosophy to their business case and bent Scrum to fit their needs.

 


 

 

 

 

Five Things Every Developer Should Know about Software Architecture (29:44)

by Simon Brown

 

 

 

 

 

In this lecture, Simon Brown will talk about software architecture. How it evolves during its lifetime, how particular decisions are critical to your architecture's future, and some other things we can figure out later and how good software architecture enables agility.
