Emoji under the hood

By Nikita Prokopov

 

 

 

 

It is often when we use things and don't think about how they work. Emojis became part of day-to-day human communication but did you ever stopped and asked yourself, "Can I use a zero-width joiner sequence to combine these two emojis to produce one that would carry the meaning of my message more precisely, or it is just a single codepoint?".

 

In this article, you will find out what makes emojis tick and what interesting things you can do with that knowledge. Also, it contains many emojis, and they will look different based on the device you're reading it from.

 


 

 

 

 

Interesting facts about Scala

by Mohammad Khalid

 

 

 

 

There is a lot of data in the world (about 1 billion TB), and it moves fast around it. (dozens of millions of transactions a minute). Systems have to scale to be able to handle increasing amounts of data. Few high-traffic websites like LinkedIn, Coursera, Duolingo, and Apache Spark, Apache Kafka, Akka utilize Scala (aka Scalable Language) for that purpose.

 

Check out this list of interesting facts about Scala to get some idea of what this language is all about. Bonus, you can hit "Next" at the bottom of the article to launch yourself into a tutorial rabbit hole.

Testing Minecraft in Minecraft  

by Henrik Kniberg

 

 

 

 

 

The difficulty of automated testing can vary depending on your application. It can be pretty easy if your architecture is decoupled correctly, and it can be insanely frustrating when a bunch of systems relies on each other. This video will share how the developers manage to automate testing Minecraft where all the systems have to be resilient to the players' actions to work.
